ElectraHub eCommerce Website Strategy
1. Homepage Layout and Content
The homepage should immediately communicate ElectraHub’s value proposition in a family-friendly,
aspirational way. Above-the-fold, feature a hero banner with a clean, high-quality image (e.g. a family
enjoying TV together) and a bold tagline like “Elevate Family Entertainment – Premium 4K TVs at Family-Friendly
Prices”. Overlay key USPs (e.g. “WebOS Smart TV | 2-Year Warranty | Free Installation | Easy EMI”) as
badges or icons near the CTA. A prominent primary CTA (e.g. “Shop TVs” or “Explore Soundbars”) should be
clearly visible. 
Below the fold, include:
- Product Categories: Visual tiles or cards for “LED TVs” and “Soundbars” with brief text.
- Trust/Value Section: Icons or text for “2-Year Warranty”, “Lifetime Service Support”, “Free Home Setup”,
“No-Cost EMIs” (reinforcing value without sounding cheap). Include industry or payment logos (UPI, Visa,
etc.) as trust badges.
- Featured Products: Highlight best-sellers or new arrivals with images and “Shop Now” links. Carousel or
grid of 3–6 items keeps it scannable.
- Social Proof: A small review snippet or “★ ★ ★ ★ ★” rating summary (e.g. average rating of a featured
TV) to hint at customer satisfaction. According to eCommerce UX research, “incorporating elements like
customer reviews, testimonials, and trust badges on the homepage enhances credibility”.
- Store Locator / Offline CTA: Since stores are opening soon, add a brief banner or section “Visit Our First
ElectraHub Store – coming [City, Date]” and a link to “Find a Store” or “Become a Dealer.”
- Newsletter/Lead Capture: A subtle footer bar or modal (non-intrusive) inviting users to “Sign up for
exclusive deals and TV tips.” 
Layout Tips: Keep navigation simple (no more than 7–8 items). Ensure the search bar is prominent in the
header (customers rely on it). The homepage is the first impression, so “users should be able to easily find
the information they need without being overwhelmed”. Use ample white space and large, clear fonts.
Above the fold, feature the unique value prop (“Premium Smart TVs. Indian Prices.”) front and center.

3. Brand Tone and Visual Language
ElectraHub’s tone should be warm, trustworthy and slightly aspirational, reflecting Indian family values.
Copy should be relatable and emotional (family, togetherness) rather than clinical. For example: “Bring
cinema-like joy to your home” or “Clear vision for every family moment”. Emphasize togetherness: “Made for
your family’s entertainment”, not just technical specs. 
Given the aspirational middle class target, language should elevate desires and convey premium
experience at a fair price. Phrases like “Upgrade your living room in style” or “Premium features, down-to-earth
pricing” resonate. Avoid overly salesy superlatives; use positive but sincere phrasing (e.g. “stunning 4K
clarity” instead of “the BEST TV ever!”). 
Visually, use a modern-minimalist design (clean layouts, sans-serif fonts, ample white space) combined
with warm accents. A neutral base (white/gray) with a distinctive accent color (e.g. a warm orange or teal)
can feel both premium and friendly. Include localized elements subtly (like a pattern inspired by Indian
fabrics or festival-theme background during Diwali), but keep it tasteful. Incorporate real (or staged) Indian
family images enjoying TV time to reinforce family-orientation – research notes that Smart TVs are now the
“digital common ground for families”, so visuals should reflect children and parents using the products
together. Consistency is key: maintain the same color palette, typography, and image style across the site
for recognition.
Copy example for homepage:
- Header tagline: “ElectraHub – Sparking Joy in Every Home”
- Banner CTA: “Experience True Cinema. Shop Now.”
- Benefit blurb: “Premium WebOS Smart TVs and Soundbars built for Indian homes – now just a click away.”
4. Header, Footer, and Navigation Structure
• Header: Use a sticky top bar with the ElectraHub logo on the left. Next to it, clear main navigation
links: TVs, Soundbars, Accessories (future: ACs, Fridges, etc.), Offers, About Us, Store Locator, 
Support/Contact. Keep menu items concise (avoid deep nesting). Place a search icon/field center or
right (users expect search at top). On the far right: user account icon, wishlist/cart icon with item
count, and a bright “Cart” button. If EMI and offers are a big draw, consider a small topmost banner
(“Easy EMI Available on All TVs”) in the header area. 
• Navigation Menus: For categories like TVs, use a dropdown or mega-menu: e.g. under TVs list
subcategories (43″, 55″, Smart TVs, etc.). Ensure hover or touch reveals options intuitively. Use 
breadcrumbs on product pages so users know their location. Test that menu works well on mobile
(hamburger menu) with clear labels.

list quick links (TVs, Soundbars, Upcoming products). Under Support: Warranty Info, Installation FAQ,
Contact Us, Return Policy. Company: About ElectraHub, Careers, Franchise Info (see Section 8). 
Connect: Blog, Newsletter signup, and social media icons (Facebook, Instagram, YouTube). Also show

- Product Page Reviews: As noted above, show star ratings and allow filtering (most recent/helpful).

In copy, avoid hard sells: use trust-building language like “100% secure checkout”, “Lifetime support”. Showing
the number of reviews or customers (e.g. “Over 5,000 TVs sold”) can also boost confidence. Overall,
“leveraging social proof such as customer reviews and testimonials […] can influence visitors to take action”.

Create content around long-tail queries (“ElectraHub 43-inch TV price in Lucknow”) for local appeal.

content or at least Hindi keyword equivalents (e.g. TV translations) – “अच्छी टीवी ब्रांड” – as this builds
trust . A bilingual site (English + Hindi) or localized landing pages (e.g. for Lucknow, Kanpur) can
capture local search traffic.

7. Payment and Checkout Strategy
The checkout flow must accommodate Indian payment habits to maximize conversions: 
• Payment Options: Lead with UPI (Google Pay, PhonePe) as the primary option – it’s ubiquitous in
India. Also offer credit/debit cards, netbanking, wallets (Paytm, etc.), and Cash-on-Delivery (COD) for
trust-strapped customers. Crucially, prominently list EMI/BNPL options: research shows 1 in 3
orders over ₹10,000 uses EMI or BNPL. Include partner logos (e.g. Bajaj Finserv, LazyPay) and an EMI
calculator in checkout. Display “No Cost EMI” if possible. 
• Price Transparency: During checkout, clearly break down costs: product total, shipping (if any),
installation (₹0), taxes. Shipping should be free (with mention of fast delivery window). Cite: “Shipping
cost transparency…reduces last-minute drop-offs”. If any shipping fee applies (unlikely on heavy goods
if company absorbs it), show it upfront on the cart page. 
• Secure UX: Use a single-page or minimal-step checkout flow. Fields should be mobile-optimized
(autofill address by PIN code). Display security badges (SSL, PCI-DSS) again at the final payment step.
Offer guest checkout to reduce friction. Provide an “Order Summary” sidebar that’s always visible. 
• Support and Trust: Include a live-chat/chatbot prompt (e.g. “Need help choosing EMI plan?”) –
evidence shows live chat and clear Q&A sections drastically reduce anxiety. Add a hotline or
WhatsApp number for assistance. Before final confirm, remind customers of the 2-year warranty and
easy returns policy (“Hassle-free 7-day returns”) for reassurance. 
• Promotions: Near the end of checkout, display gentle prompts: “Congratulations, you’re eligible for
No-Cost EMI!” or “Free installation included – Save ₹999”. Use coupon fields visibly for any running
promotions (e.g. “NEWYEAR10 for 10% off”). 
• After-Sale: On confirmation page/email, provide order tracking info and customer support contacts.
Show appreciation (“Thank you for choosing ElectraHub!”) to encourage positive sentiment.

and mismatched payment flows cause drop-off, so every button and field must be big, fast, and error-free.

9. Increasing Conversions (Subtle Tactics)
Personalization: Show “Recommended for you” or “Customers also viewed” product carousels based on
browsing. This gently nudges additional interest. Allow wishlisting so visitors can save items and
later receive reminders (e.g. email alerts on price drops or stock). 
Limited-Time Messaging: Use tasteful urgency (e.g. a banner: “Diwali Sale – 20% off until Oct 31st”).
On product pages, you might show “15 people are viewing this” or “Only 5 left in stock” as micro-FOMO.
However, avoid aggressive pop-ups: over 82% of users dislike interruptive popups. Instead, use inline
banners or exit-intent banners with a small offer (e.g. “Wait! Get ₹500 off on checkout”). 
Interactive Elements: Implement a “Quick View” modal on category pages: when hovering over a
TV, a small window shows key specs and “Add to Cart” without leaving the page. This reduces clicks
friction . 
Mobile-First CTAs: Ensure “Add to Cart” and “Buy Now” buttons are large enough and thumbreachable on phones (94% of users are on mobile). 
Content Up-sells: On product pages or checkout, suggest complementary items (“Complete the
Experience: Add a Soundbar for ₹X off”). Present these as helpful combos, not pushy pop-ups. 
Social Proof Everywhere: Incorporate small trust cues on category pages too (e.g. average rating
below a product tile). According to UX research, even small review snippets greatly affect
conversions. 
Post-Purchase Engagement: After checkout, prompt customers to share their purchase (e.g. “We’ll
notify you of delivery via WhatsApp!”). Later, email for reviews or referrals (“Refer a friend and both get
a ₹500 gift card”). This builds loyalty without upfront pressure. 
Throughout, maintain a friendly, advisory tone (“Need help choosing? Chat with us!”) rather than hardselling. Every conversion tactic (urgency, upsell, recommendations) should feel helpful. Remember: “Keep
the design simple…avoid jargon and keep text straightforward”, so nothing confuses the customer. With this
subtle, user-centric approach, ElectraHub can gently guide shoppers toward purchase without feeling
pushy.
